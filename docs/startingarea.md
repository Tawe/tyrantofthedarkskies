# **Starting Area – New Cove & The Black Anchor**

  

This document defines the **starting area** for new characters: the port town of **New Cove** and its surrounding region. This area introduces players to the world’s history, factions, mechanics, and tone while providing clear early hooks for exploration and advancement.

---

## **World Context: The Refound Continent**

  

The continent on which New Cove stands is believed to be the **birthplace of life itself**. Ancient records speak of vast empires that rose and fell here long before recorded history elsewhere. A **great tragedy** — the nature of which remains unclear — caused the land to be abandoned, forcing survivors to flee across the ocean.

  

Only recently has the continent been **rediscovered**.

  

New Cove is currently the **only known settlement** on this land.

---

## **New Cove (Port Town)**

  

New Cove is a young, pragmatic settlement built quickly for survival rather than beauty. Wooden structures dominate, reinforced by scavenged stone and shipwreck materials. The town exists at the edge of the known world.

  

### **Key Characteristics**

- Population: Small, transient, and growing
    
- Primary purpose: Exploration, trade, and staging expeditions inland
    
- Governance: Appointed Governor representing distant interests
    
- Defense: Wooden palisade, town barracks, limited militia
    

---

## **The Black Anchor (Starting Location)**

  

**The Black Anchor** is the first location all players enter.

  

### **Description**

  

A squat, weather-beaten tavern built from dark driftwood and iron fittings reclaimed from wrecked ships. The smell of salt, ale, and wet rope fills the air. Lanterns sway constantly, even when there is no wind.

  

### **Functions**

- Primary entertainment venue
    
- Tavern and lodging
    
- **Official Adventurers’ Guild Hall** for New Cove
    

  

### **Adventurers’ Guild Role**

- Companies, nobles, merchants, and wealthy elites post **contracts and bounties** here
    
- Jobs focus on:
    
    - Exploration of the wilderness
        
    - Securing trade routes
        
    - Recovering relics of lost civilizations
        
    - Dealing with unknown creatures
        
    

  

### **Gameplay Purpose**

- Character onboarding
    
- Tutorial-style quests
    
- Party formation hub
    
- Social and faction introduction

## **Locations Within New Cove**

  

### **Temple of Lumeris —** 

### **The Hall of Change**

  

A serene structure of pale stone and flowing water channels.

  

**Functions**

- Spiritual communion
    
- Healing and blessings
    
- Religious maneuvers tied to **Lumeris**
    
- Meditation and reflection
    

  

**Faction Presence**

- Followers of Lumeris
    
- Neutral ground respected by most factions
    

---

### **Jalia’s Goods (General Store)**

  

A cramped but meticulously organized shop.

  

**Stock**

- Basic weapons and armor
    
- Tools and supplies
    
- Rations, rope, lamps
    
- Limited rotating inventory
    

  

**Notes**

- Early economy hub
    
- Prices influenced by NPC & Faction View Matrix
    

---

### **Magitium Outpost**

  

A compact stone-and-brass structure flying the sigil of the **Magitium Consortium** — a guild of merchant mages.

  

**Functions**

- Sale of magical tomes and scrolls
    
- Minor enchanted items
    
- Arcane services (identify, minor enchantments)
    

  

**Notes**

- Limited access for low-reputation players
    
- Introduces magical economy and lore
    

---

### **City Barracks**

  

Home to New Cove’s guards and militia.

  

**Functions**

- Law enforcement
    
- Combat training
    
- Guard-related contracts
    

---

### **Governor’s House**

  

A fortified wooden manor overlooking the docks.

  

**Functions**

- Political authority
    
- High-level contracts
    
- Faction negotiations
    

---

### **The Docks**

  

Constantly active with ships arriving and departing.

  

**Functions**

- Travel and trade
    
- Sea-based contracts
    
- Introduction to seafaring dangers
    

---

### **The Wooden Wall**

  

A hastily constructed palisade surrounding New Cove.

  

**Notes**

- Symbol of fragility and frontier living
    
- Regularly tested by wildlife and unknown threats
    

---

## **Outside the Walls: The Unflooded Sea**

  

Beyond the palisade lies the **Unflooded Sea** — a vast plain of ancient ocean floor that has risen to sea level.

  

### **Terrain Features**

- **Rift Forests**: Twisted coral-like growths and stone spires
    
- **Kelp Plains**: Vast fields of hardened kelp fronds waving in the wind
    

  

### **Known & Rumored Inhabitants**

- Crabfolk said to **farm snails** using ancient techniques
    
- Unmapped ruins buried beneath silt and coral stone
    
- Strange tides with no water
    

  

### **Gameplay Role**

- Primary early exploration zone
    
- Environmental hazards
    
- First contact with non-human civilizations
    
- Source of early crafting materials
    

---

## **Design Intent**

- Establish tone: frontier, mystery, lost history
    
- Introduce all major systems organically
    
- Encourage exploration over linear questing
    
- Make New Cove feel temporary, fragile, and alive
    

  

The world does not wait for the players — it reacts to them.

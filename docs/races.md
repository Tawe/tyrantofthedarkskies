# Playable Species

This document defines the playable species of the world, including their cultural identity and mechanical attribute modifiers. Attribute modifiers apply at character creation.

---

## Humans

**Description**  
Humans are adaptable generalists found throughout the world. Their cultures are diverse, and their greatest strength is flexibility rather than specialization.

**Cultural Traits**

- Highly adaptable
    
- Diverse beliefs and professions
    
- No single dominant culture
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+2 Attribute Points** freely assigned at character creation
    
- No inherent penalties
    

---

## Sharah

**Description**  
The Sharah are large, half-giant, rock-skinned humans who dwell in the high mountains. Their culture is deeply druidic, centered on stone, weather, and the long memory of the earth. They are slow to anger, but terrifying when roused.

**Cultural Traits**

- Mountain-dwelling
    
- Druidic traditions
    
- Patient, enduring, and stoic
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+1 Physical**
    
- **+1 Spiritual**
    
- **-1 Social**
    

---

## Gal’tar (Hobgoblins)

**Description**  
The Gal’tar are a disciplined, militaristic people who live by a strict code of honor, hierarchy, and service. Their society values obedience, preparation, and mastery of arms, blending martial rigor with philosophical restraint.

**Cultural Traits**

- Highly regimented society
    
- Code-driven honor culture
    
- Strong emphasis on training and discipline
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+1 Physical**
    
- **+1 Mental**
    
- **-1 Social**
    

---

## Dwarves

**Description**  
Unlike their landbound cousins of other worlds, these Dwarves are a seafaring people with an almost spiritual call to the ocean. They are known for their warmth, storytelling, and strong communal bonds.

**Cultural Traits**

- Seafaring and coastal settlements
    
- Strong oral traditions
    
- Open and social communities
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+1 Physical**
    
- **+1 Social**
    
- **-1 Spiritual**
    

---

## Toraelqui

**Description**  
The Toraelqui are a simian, aquatic race who worship the Great Underflow — a vast, unseen current believed to guide all waters and all lives. Their spirituality is experiential, shaped by tides, pressure, and motion rather than dogma.

**Cultural Traits**

- Aquatic and amphibious
    
- Strong ritual traditions tied to water
    
- Communal, flow-oriented worldview
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+1 Spiritual**
    
- **+1 Physical**
    
- **-1 Social**
    

---

## Jalitic (Orcs)

**Description**  
The Jalitic are an urban, scholarly race created through experiments conducted by an ancient wizard. Designed to survive and adapt, they became meticulous thinkers, archivists, and engineers rather than soldiers.

**Cultural Traits**

- Urban and scholarly
    
- Strong traditions of record-keeping and civic systems
    
- Pragmatic, curious, and analytical
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+1 Mental**
    
- **+1 Social**
    
- **-1 Physical**
    

---

## Tuskagor

**Description**  
The Tuskagor are a cursed race of hogfolk, born from a divine punishment. Long ago, an ancient kingdom attempted to purge the followers of the Goddess of Food. The curse twisted the persecutors into what they sought to destroy, binding hunger, strength, and shame into their bloodline.

**Cultural Traits**

- Strong physical presence
    
- Cultural memory of divine punishment
    
- Deeply conflicted relationship with food and faith
    

**Mechanical Traits**

- **+2 Physical**
    
- **-1 Mental**
